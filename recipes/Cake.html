Sponge Cake

Sponge cake is a light and airy baked sweet blast

Sponge cake is a classic and versatile baked good that is popular in many countries around the world. It is made by whipping eggs and sugar together until they are light and fluffy, and then gently folding in flour. This creates a batter that is delicate, airy, and has a spongy texture. The cake is then baked in an oven, resulting in a light and fluffy finished product. Sponge cake can be enjoyed on its own, or used as the base for various desserts such as trifles, jelly rolls, or Charlotte. It can also be flavored with various ingredients such as vanilla, lemon, or chocolate.

There are several types of sponge cake, but the most common are Genoise and angel food cake. Genoise is a traditional French sponge cake that is made with butter, while angel food cake is an American sponge cake that is made without butter, using only the whipped egg whites for leavening. Both are considered to be light, airy and spongy, and can be flavored and decorated in various ways. The sponge cakes are often used for making layered cakes, roll cakes and trifles.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ¼ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons butter, room temperature
  • ½ cup flaked coconut
  • ¼ cup chopped walnuts
  • 4 tablespoons milk

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9-inch square pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, beat eggs for 4 minutes with electric mixer. Gradually add sugar; beat until light and fluffy, 4 to 5 minutes.
  3. Whisk together the flour and baking powder, and add to egg mixture until just combined.
  4. In a saucepan, heat 1/2 cup milk and 2 tablespoons butter until butter is melted. Gradually pour into the batter, mixing constantly. Pour into prepared pan.
  5. Bake in preheated oven until cake is golden and a toothpick inserted in the center of the cake comes out clean, 20 to 25 minutes.
  6. Remove cake from oven; preheat oven's broiler.
  7. Beat 1/4 cup brown sugar and 2 tablespoons butter until combined. Stir in milk to desired consistency. Stir in coconut and nuts. Spread over warm cake. Broil 4 inches from heat for 3 to 4 minutes, till golden.
